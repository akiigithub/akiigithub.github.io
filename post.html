WHAT IS STD?

May 3rd 2023 2 Min read

Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs), also known as sexually transmitted infections (STIs), are very common. STDs pass from one person to another through vaginal, oral, and anal sex. They also can spread through intimate physical contact like heavy petting, though this is not very common. STDs donâ€™t always cause symptoms or may only cause mild symptoms. Therefore, it is possible to have an infection and not know it. That is why getting an STD test is important if you are having sex. If you receive a positive STD diagnosis, know that all are treatable with medicine and some are curable entirely. Some STDs can be passed from a pregnant person to the baby, either during pregnancy or when giving birth. Other ways that STDs may be spread include during breastfeeding, through blood transfusions, or by sharing needles. There are dozens of STDs and two of the common STDs are Chlamydia and Genitals Warts.
